Landmark Trials

HYVET (2008) — Hypertension in the Very Elderly

Antihypertensive therapy in patients ≥80 years reduced stroke (30%) and heart failure (64%) with indapamide ± perindopril.

Practice-changing NEJM

SPRINT (2015) — Intensive BP Target

SBP target <120 vs <140 mmHg reduced CV events and mortality in high-risk adults; subgroup analyses include older adults with careful monitoring for AEs.

High impact NEJM

STOPP/START v2 — Potentially Inappropriate Prescribing

Structured criteria to identify medications to stop or start in older adults; improves prescribing quality and reduces ADRs.

Core geriatrics

Deprescribing

  1. Identify high-risk medications (anticholinergics, benzos, Z-drugs, antipsychotics when not indicated).
  2. Assess indication, time-to-benefit, and patient goals of care.
  3. Plan taper schedules; monitor withdrawal and symptom recurrence.
  4. Communicate clearly with patients and caregivers; provide written plans.
